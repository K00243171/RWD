Public asked to support Shannon Airport as travel industry reopens


People are being asked to support Shannon Airport while the travel industry begins to reboot as restrictions are lifted.

The government is to consider recommendations by an interim report by the Aviation Recovery Taskforce on air travel.
It recommends that air travel restrictions should be lifted and the 14 day quarantine period should also be abandoned by July 1st.
Tony Brazil of Limerick Travel says even with these recommendations implemented Shannon Airport will still need a lot of support to recover.
As it stands, easing of the COVID-19 restrictions on 29 June (Phase 3) includes:
Being able to travel anywhere in Ireland.

The re-opening of creches, childminding facilities and pre-schools as well as outdoor/indoor play areas.

Passengers arriving from overseas are expected to self-isolate for 14 days and provide proof of where they were isolating.
